### The problem we're solving

A walk-in or freezer that fails overnight isn't found until the morning shift opens the door —
and by then the load is gone. A single failure can spoil **$2,000–$10,000+** of product, plus the
lost prep and the scramble to re-stock. Most kitchens still check temps by hand on a clipboard,
which means nobody is watching the hours when failures actually happen.

### What we'll install (at no cost to you)

- A small **monitoring hub** (free software) on a back-office computer you already keep on.
- **[N] wireless temperature probes** — one in each unit you want covered (walk-in cooler,
  walk-in freezer, reach-ins, prep line).
- Each unit gets its own **safe high/low range**, pre-set for cooler vs. freezer.

### What you get

- **Alerts in minutes** — email and/or text (via your team's phone or Slack) the moment a unit
  drifts out of range, day or night. A cracked door, a dying compressor, an iced coil — you hear
  about it while the food is still good.
- **One screen** showing every cooler's live temperature and history.
- **A silent-sensor alert** — if a probe loses power or Wi-Fi, that silence is itself an alarm, so
  a dark freezer never passes as "fine."
- **An automatic temperature log** you can export to a spreadsheet any time — less clipboard time
  for your staff.

### What this is — and isn't (so there are no surprises)

- It's an **operational monitoring and loss-prevention tool**. It is **not** a certified,
  NIST-traceable, or HACCP/health-code compliance instrument, and it does **not** replace your
  required manual checks or official records — you and your food-safety plan stay responsible for those.
- Everything runs on power: a **full power outage** stops monitoring (the coolers are off too). We
  can put the hub and router on a small battery backup if that matters to you.

- **Your honest feedback** at the end — did it catch anything, was it easy, what would you pay for it.
