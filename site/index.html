How it works

From probe to alert in three steps.

1

Place the probe

Drop the waterproof stainless probe wherever temperature matters — a fridge, a tank, a room, a rack. Add a humidity sensor on the expansion header if you need it.

2

Join your Wi-Fi

Power on and connect in the browser — no app-store download to get started. Setpoint remembers the network and reconnects on its own.

3

Set the line, walk away

Choose your safe range. Watch it live on the dashboard, and let the alerts do the watching when you can't.

FAQ

Before you buy.

Do I need a cloud account or a subscription?

No. Setpoint reports to a free app on your own machine over your local network — no account, no telemetry, nothing phoning home. The internet is only touched if you choose to let email alerts leave your network.

What computer do I need to run it?

Any always-on Windows or Linux machine you already keep on — a mini-PC, NAS, home server, or a Raspberry Pi. That local box is what stores your history and fires your alerts without a cloud. If you don't already leave a machine running, Setpoint isn't the right fit.

How accurate is it?

±0.5 °C typical, uncalibrated (you can add a per-probe offset in the app). Great for "is my rack, fridge, or freezer in range and trending safe" — it is not a certified, NIST-traceable, or medical instrument.

Wi-Fi and setup?

2.4 GHz only (no 5 GHz). Setup happens on the device in your browser — join the probe's temporary network, pick yours, done. No phone app to install.

What about the battery?

The portable build runs on one 18650 cell (not included — lithium shipping rules) and recharges over USB-C. Or run it always-on from USB with no cell at all. The DIY kit is cell-not-included; add any reputable protected or flat-top 18650.

Is the firmware open?

Yes — read it, build it, and reflash it from your browser any time. You're never locked into a vendor or a server that can be switched off.
